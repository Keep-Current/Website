What will I learn?

You will learn the practical details of deep learning applications with hands-on model building using PyTorch and fast.ai and work on problems ranging from computer vision, natural language processing, and recommendation systems.

After finishing this course you be able to:

  • apply transfer learning to image classification problems
  • use neural networks for recommendation algorithms
  • use recurrent neural networks and convolutional neural networks for text classification problems
  • apply neural networks to tabular data and learn embeddings for categorical variables
  • Previous experience programming in Python and some machine learning background is advised to make best use of the course.

In addition, we will dive deeper into the theory behind the practice, and enable you to deeply understand the algorithms as well as to dare to tweak, hack and change them for better performance and usage.

Format and Timeline

The course is made of 7 meetings. Before each meeting, you are expected to watch the course video on your free time, and arrive to the meeting with insights and questions. The questions will be answered by other members in the studying-group. Thus, enabling members to learn and understand better by teaching others. As we all come from different backgrounds, we will enrich each other by expressing and emphasizing the differnet aspects of machine learning algorithms in practice and theory.

The first meeting will be held in February. We will meet 3 times a month, in the evening, after work, with intervals of 1-2 weeks between every meeting.

What is expected from me?

Unfortunately, this course is not open for everyone.

You are expected to have at least 2 years experience with programming and mathematical background. Preferably, you should feel comfortable with programming in Python as well as having good knowledge in Calculus and Linear Algebra. A mathematical oriented university degree (Computer science, Physics, Mathematics, etc.) is a huge plus.
